Pillars of "Bangladesh 2.0"

Innovation

Bangladesh innovates in microfinance, ready-made garments, digital services, renewable energy, rural healthcare, sustainable farming, and disaster management technologies.

Sustainability

Bangladesh focuses on renewable energy, climate adaptation, sustainable agriculture, green technology, and community-driven initiatives to ensure environmental and economic sustainability.

Infrastructure

Bangladesh's infrastructure includes expanding road networks, bridges like Padma Bridge, ports, railways, energy projects, and growing urban developments.

Empowerment

Bangladesh empowers communities through microfinance, women's education, digital inclusion, entrepreneurship, healthcare access, and social equity initiatives, fostering progress and resilience.

The Vision of Future

Bangladesh 2.0 aims to redefine the nation’s trajectory by embracing cutting-edge technology, promoting inclusive growth, and ensuring sustainability. With initiatives in renewable energy, digital transformation, and robust infrastructure, the country is set to become a global model of development.

Digital Transformation

Creating a nationwide digital ecosystem to enhance connectivity and access to services for all citizens.

Green Energy

Investments in solar and wind energy to ensure a sustainable and eco-friendly future.

Modern Infrastructure

Building state-of-the-art infrastructure to support economic growth and global competitiveness.

"Bangladesh 2.0 is our opportunity to create a nation that inspires the world. We must focus on eliminating poverty, unemployment, and carbon emissions. Through social businesses, we can solve problems sustainably, empowering communities while protecting the environment. Our youth hold the key to this transformation, armed with innovation, creativity, and determination. Let us prioritize renewable energy, sustainable agriculture, and inclusive technologies to build a resilient future. Bangladesh has always been a land of possibilities. Together, we can redefine our destiny, showing the world how to achieve economic prosperity and social justice while preserving our planet for generations to come."

Dr. Muhammad Yunus

Chief Adviser of the People's Republic of Bangladesh

July uprising: Conspiracy, greed causing rift in unity

Date: 11/01/2025 Time: 12:00 AM

Jamaat-e-Islami Secretary General Mia Golam Parwar yesterday said a conspiracy and greed are causing a rift in the unity among students and the public that existed during the July uprising. He made this remark while speaking at a workers' conference held at Bhabanipur Muktijoddha College ground in Gazipur Sadar Upazila.

Bangladesh plans to hold elections in late 2025 or early 2026

Date: 16/12/2024 Time: 11:00 PM

General elections in Bangladesh will be held in late 2025 or early 2026, the country’s interim leader Muhammad Yunus, who heads the caretaker government installed after a popular revolution in August, announced.
“Election dates could be fixed by the end of 2025 or the first half of 2026,” the Nobel Peace Prize-winning leader said in a national broadcast on Monday delivered on the 53rd anniversary of Bangladesh winning independence.
